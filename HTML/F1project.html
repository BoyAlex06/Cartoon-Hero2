Formula 1 cars of the 2023 season

Red Bull Racing



The Red Bull Racing RB19 is a Formula One car designed and constructed by Red Bull Racing competing in the 2023 Formula One World Championship1. The car was unveiled in New York City on 3 February 20231. The RB19 is driven by defending world champion Max Verstappen and Sergio Pérez. The car features heavily refined aerodynamics, with clear changes to the shape of the nose, sidepods and engine cover – including an airbox.

Mercedes AMG Petronas



The Mercedes W14 is a Formula One racing car designed and constructed by the Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula One Team competing in the 2023 Formula One World Championship1. The car was designed with a higher ride height with the aim to reduce porpoising. However, the car proved to be less competitive than expected at the start of the season, causing key figures in the Mercedes team to question the effectiveness of the car’s concept. The W14 features a striking black livery, used by Mercedes through 2020 and 2021 as part of its anti-racism messaging

Scuderia Ferrari



The Ferrari SF-23 (also known by its internal name, Project 675) is a Formula One racing car designed and constructed by Scuderia Ferrari competing in the 2023 Formula One World Championship. The car is driven by Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz Jr. car was launched on 14 February 2023 and was revealed as SF-23 on 7 February 2023. The SF-23 shows a great resemblance to the F1-75 that preceded it, but in reality it is a car that should have corrected the errors that had made it weak from a certain point of the season onwards. The SF-23 is a considerable evolution of last year’s chassis. The SF-23 sidepods compared with F1-75’s the depressions in the sidepod top remain and there is no Red Bull-like cooling cannon in the bodywork sides, the hot air extraction taken care of by the extensive array of louvres

Aston Martin Cognizant Formula 1 Team



The Aston Martin AMR23 is a Formula One racing car designed and developed by the Aston Martin F1 team competing in the 2023 Formula One World Championship. It is the third Formula One car entered by Aston Martin in the 21st century, and is driven by Lance Stroll and Fernando Alonso. The car has a carbon fibre composite monocoque chassis with Zylon legality side anti-intrusion panels. The suspension consists of aluminium uprights with carbon fibre composite wishbones, track rod and pushrod. Inboard chassis mounted torsion springs, dampers and anti-roll bar assembly are also present.
The AMR23 is a considerable evolution of last year’s chassis, which was honed across the season into a consistent points challenger. In year three of the team’s ambitious journey as Aston Martin, the aim is to deliver a car that will be competitive from the outset

Mclaren Racing



The McLaren MCL60 is a Formula One car designed and constructed by McLaren under the direction of James Key to compete in the 2023 Formula One World Championship1. The car made its competitive début at the 2023 Bahrain Grand Prix. The MCL60 features a striking papaya orange and blue livery, the colours first carried by the team in the 1960s, which have since become a team favourite since their reintroduction in 2018. The car is driven by Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri.

Alpine Renault



The Alpine A523 is a Formula One racing car designed and constructed by the BWT Alpine F1 Team for the 2023 Formula One World Championship. The car is driven by Pierre Gasly, in his first season for the Enstone-based team, and Esteban Ocon. The A523 appears visually similar to the team’s 2022 car, but sports a new front wing design and a nose cone with a flatter upper section. It also features more bulbous rear bodywork around its engine cover – made more identifiable by the use of black paint in this region.
